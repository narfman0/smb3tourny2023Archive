MwdaShadow 01/26/2023 12:41 AM
For the Zelda II tournament last fall, we did a Swiss Rounds format for the prelims, to narrow down the field of 34 to a 16 player bracket. To the extent possible, players were always grouped and randomly matched by record: Round 1: all 17 matchups were randomly drawn from a hat Round 2: Since there were an odd number of matches, the R1 winner with the weakest time faced the R1 loser with the best time. Then all other 1-0 players and 0-1 players were put in separate pools and matches drawn from a hat (8 matches in each pool) Round 3: There were eight 2-0 players, eighteen 1-1 players, and eight 0-2 players. Each were put in their respective pools and drawn from a hat Round 4: The four remaining 3-0 and 0-3 players were randomly divided up into two games in their respective pools. There were 13 each in the 2-1 and 1-2 pools. The 1-2 player with the best average time faced the 2-1 player with the weakest average time. The other players were divided up into six more matches in each pool Round 5: The two remaining 4-0 players faced off for the top seed, the eight 3-1 players were randomly grouped into 4 matches (mostly for seeding with the outside chance of an elimination), the eighteen 2-2 players were grouped into 9 matches that were basically win to get in, and the remaining eight 1-3 players and two 0-4 players were grouped up into consolation matches The way it worked out, we had a great turnout and one of the 3-2 players were going to be left out of the brackets. I don't think we anticipated so many players, so the format was chosen with the expectation that a few 2-3 players would make it in. Of course, it could still work out that way in this example if you decided, say, to increase the field for the brackets to 24. The top 8 players would get byes, and some of those players who were 1-3 could still possibly be in it, as seven of those players who finished 2-3 would get in. Ties were broken by average time, with a DNF being given an automatic 3 hour time
MwdaShadow 01/26/2023 12:49 AM
This format worked well to make sure everyone was able to play at least five games. As it turned out, one of the players who qualified for the brackets dropped out due to a conflict, so no 3-2 players missed the cut. Another player wound up not being able to play due to a weather event and basically became a random bye for one of the winless players (and a guaranteed win for the other final 0-4 player). There were some other random games where players had to forfeit due to technical issues and such, but players would still get their games in to get a time toward their average, usually just tagging along with another scheduled race. It also wouldn't necessarily have to be so long as 5 rounds. The format could work with fewer rounds; wherever it's cut off, you would pare down to the brackets by record and then average time. No one necessarily even needs to be cut at all. The prelim could just be used for seeding everyone into a bracket, and making sure everyone gets to play in a few races.
